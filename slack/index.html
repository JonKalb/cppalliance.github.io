C++ Language Slack Workspace

This is a multi-channel chat environment which gathers people using C++ worldwide, into the same place so we can all learn from each other. It is like IRC but with modern clients that support multimedia, notifications, and smart devices.

FAQ

  • Why do you need my e-mail?

    Slack is invite based. Your e-mail address is used to register you in the workspace. The invitation tool does not store your address.

  • Who operates this Slack workspace?

    This workspace is paid for and operated in public by The C++ Alliance in accordance with its mission to promote the general welfare of the C++ programming language.

  • What are the rules?

    Behavior should remain relatively professional in public channels, as people from all over the world meet here to discuss C++ topics. We ask that you follow the Code of Conduct.

  • How are the rules enforced?

    Due to the sheer size of the community, it is not practical for admins to police every channel all the time. When you see a user acting badly, consider letting them know in a nice way. Give a benefit of the doubt generously, as spirited debate is sometimes mistaken for bad behavior.

    If diplomacy fails and disruptions persist, please contact one of the admins privately and state the nature of the problem.

Code of Conduct

  1. Friendly cooperation

    Harassment, insults, discrimination and cyberbullying have no place here.

  2. No excessive self-promotion

    A link to your project(s) is of course welcome - you can even present your latest task at your company in detail. Howewer keep in mind that this community is not intended to be an advertising platform.

  3. Job offers

    Job offers are fine, but please include the location, the company, a link to details and keep it in the relevant room.
